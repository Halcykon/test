Transforming Telehealth in a post-COVID world


This project is under NDA.
For a detailed case study, methodology, and outcomes discussion, please email me.

THE CONTEXT

Mayo built infrastructure to accommodate telehealth during COVID, and now wants to streamline operations

Mayo Clinic rapidly scaled their telehealth offerings to accommodate the COVID quarantine. As Senior Product Designer, I was tasked to perform a comprehensive evaluation of Mayo's Video Visits. 

I led comprehensive user research across 38+ stakeholders (physicians, patients, and operational staff) to understand the complete service ecosystem behind virtual care delivery.

Scope
Research Timeline: 6 months (2021)
Implementation Timeline: 2021-2023
Departments: Across 11 medical specialties
Geography: 4 locations

THE CHALLENGE

How do we transform a rapidly-scaled telehealth platform from "functional" to exceptional? 

Despite high patient satisfaction scores, operational metrics revealed significant friction in increasing support costs, provider burnout, and patient abandonment in critical pre-appointment flows.

THE APPROACH

Conducted deep qualitative research, synthesized 550+ insights, built stakeholder alignment across physician groups and IT teams, and developed a 2-year product roadmap balancing quick wins with platform investments.

BUSINESS IMPACT

● 30% reduction in patient abandonment (below industry average)
● 27% increase in conversion rates (exceeded targets)
● Significant ($MM) annual cost savings through operational efficiency
● 93% satisfaction for new product offerings
● Strategic product roadmap that continues to guide platform evolution
